Meridian:
Local Payments. Anywhere.

Meridian is a local-to-local payment infrastructure layer. Senders stay in domestic apps, merchants receive local currency, and Solana handles settlement orchestration under the hood. No retail crypto UX required.

The Problem

Local apps are great.
Until you leave.

The world has amazing local payment apps. But they're islands. They don't talk to each other. So the moment you cross a border, you're stuck.

🏝️

Every app is an island

Twint, Pix, iDEAL, GCash, they all work brilliantly at home. Cross a border and they're useless.

💸

Fallback = expensive

So people fall back to cards and wire transfers. That means 3 to 6% fees, slow settlement, and a bad experience.

🧠

Crypto is too much friction

Existing crypto solutions ask users to learn wallets, seeds, and gas. Most people won't. That's a dead end.

Why Blockchain?

Why not just use
banks for this?

Fair question. The short answer: traditional rails weren't built for this. Solana + stablecoins solve the exact problems that make cross-border payments expensive and slow.

01

No prefunded corridors

Banks need money sitting idle in accounts in every country. With stablecoins, we don't. That's a massive operational cost gone.

02

Near-instant settlement

Solana finalizes in under a second. Bank cross-border wires can take 1 to 3 days. That's not infrastructure, that's a liability.

03

Programmable rules

Smart contracts on Solana let us encode settlement logic, compliance checks, and routing, all on-chain, auditable, and automatic.

04

Neutral bridge asset

Stablecoins are the universal translator between currencies. No single country's bank is in the middle taking a cut.
